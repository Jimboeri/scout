Scout membership database user documentation

Introduction

The Scout Database system has been written to assist leaders at all levels within the scout organisation to manage details about people involved in scouting. It grew initially out of a desire to create a membership management system that would allow leaders to manage thier own members without needing a central person to do it all. The next challenge was to manage badges and awards, especially in cubs.

Structure of the database

The database is structured so that any group in New Zealand could use it if they wanted to, and leaders at district, area and national level could also get useful information in the future. It is arranged as follows:

National level (Scouting NZ)  
Areas  
Districts  
Groups  
Units within the groups (cub packs etc)  

People

The database stores each person associated with scouting as its own identity. A scout/cub/kea etc is stored once and their parents each have thir own identity, with a relationship existing between junior members (keas, cubs scouts etc) and their parents. Chaning the address of a parent for example changes it for all their children. This also enable parents that get more involved to be defined as leaders without re-entering any information, they are justr assigned the role that is appropriate to them.
